Operations Guide
How to use every feature. Written for everyone.

This guide walks through every screen in TRACE, what it does, and how to use it. Start with the overview, then explore whatever interests you.

TRACE has two sides: a phone app for reporters in the field, and a desktop console for operators managing the chapter. This guide covers both.

01

The big picture

Someone in the field spots a vehicle. They open the app, type the plate, describe what they saw, and tap submit. That report lands in the operator's triage queue within seconds. The operator decides what to do: track it, dispatch someone to check it out, or dismiss it with a note back to the reporter.

Reporter spots vehicle → Submits sighting → Operator reviews → Dispatch or track → Reporters respond

Over time, sightings build a picture. The operator sees which vehicles keep showing up, where they go, and when. Vehicles get escalated through concern levels. People associated with vehicles get their own profiles. When something becomes a real incident, there is a full evidence system for documenting it.

02

The reporter app

Reporters use the phone app. They don't sign up with email or phone numbers. Instead, you give them a one-time invite code in person (or over Signal). Once they enter the code, they're in.

Submitting a sighting

Type the license plate, describe what you see, and the app grabs your GPS location automatically. Tap submit and you're done. The whole thing takes about 15 seconds. If you lose signal, the report saves on your phone and sends itself when you're back online.

Checking a plate

Type any plate to check if it's already in the system. If it matches a tracked vehicle, you see a one-tap button to file a full sighting report.

Responding to dispatches

When the operator drops a pin on the map, it shows up on every reporter's phone. Tap the pin to let the operator know you're heading there. Your status updates automatically as you respond, arrive, and complete.

Reporting harassment

If someone is calling or texting to harass you, report the number through the Alert tab. The operator sees how many reporters flagged the same number and can tag it or respond with guidance.

03

Triage and dispatch

Every sighting lands in a queue. You work through them one by one. Each sighting shows if the plate matches a vehicle you're already tracking (MATCH) or if it's brand new (NEW PLATE).

Keyboard shortcuts make triage fast. Press ? anywhere in the console to see them all. The essentials: C confirm, D dismiss, F flag, N next, P previous.

Confirming a sighting

When you confirm, you pick what kind of event it is (suspicious vehicle, welfare check, follow-up), how urgent it is, and write a short note that reporters will see on their map. A dispatch pin appears on everyone's phone immediately.

Dismissing a sighting

Maybe it's a resident's car, a delivery truck, or a duplicate report. Dismiss it with a brief reason and the reporter gets that feedback so they know their report was reviewed.

04

Activity Map

Every sighting plotted on a satellite map. This is where patterns emerge. Right-click anywhere to drop a dispatch pin that shows up on every reporter's phone.

Heatmap

Shows where sightings are concentrated. Red spots mean lots of activity. Use this to identify problem intersections or areas that need more patrols.

Corridors

Pick a tracked vehicle and see every place it's been spotted, connected by a line showing its movement path. This reveals routes and habits.

Time playback

Drag the slider to replay sightings over time. Spot patterns like a vehicle that shows up every Tuesday evening, or activity that spikes on weekends.

Co-occurrence

Finds vehicles that keep showing up in the same place at the same time. If two cars always appear together, there might be a connection worth investigating.

05

Vehicles and actors

These are your intelligence records. Vehicles and people build up over time as more sightings come in.

Concern levels

Every tracked vehicle has a concern level that shows how worried you are about it. You decide when to move a vehicle up or down. The system logs every change with a reason and timestamp.

NoticedSeen once, nothing suspicious yet
MonitoringShowed up a few times, keeping an eye on it
Active ConcernClear pattern, actively tracking
Priority WatchHighest priority, respond immediately
These level names are examples. In Admin you can rename them, add new ones, remove old ones, and set colors. Make the ladder match your chapter's language.

Vehicle details

Click any vehicle to see everything about it: plate, make, model, color, what types it's tagged as (sedan, SUV, pickup), its concern level history, its last 20 sightings with locations, and any people linked to it.

Actors

An actor is a person observed in connection with a vehicle. You only store aliases (code names) and physical descriptions. Never real names. If a person switches cars, all their vehicles are linked to the same profile. The actor record outlives any individual vehicle.

06

Incidents and evidence

When something goes from "we're watching this" to "this is a real problem," you create an incident. An incident pulls together vehicles, people, and evidence into one documented case.

Create incident → Link vehicles & people → Collect evidence → Get witness statements → Close or escalate

Evidence phases

Evidence moves through four stages: Collection (photos, notes, timestamps), Processing (organizing what you have), Analysis (connecting the dots), and Presentation (creating a document you can hand to someone).

Witness forms

Need a statement from someone outside your reporter network? Generate a link and text it to them. They open it in a browser, describe what they saw, and submit. No app needed, no account needed. The link only works for 48 hours and stops accepting responses after 20 submissions.

Printable records

Every incident has a "Print Record" button that generates a clean document you can print to PDF. Use it for police reports, insurance claims, HOA meetings, or community presentations.

Evidence is stored securely. If you set an encryption key in your server settings, all evidence files are scrambled with military-grade encryption (AES-256) before being saved. Without the key, the files are unreadable.
07

Safety and security

TRACE is built for situations where the people doing the reporting could be at risk. Everything protects reporter identities.

No real names in the database

TRACE uses three separate data vaults. The main vault stores everything operators work with: vehicles, sightings, dispatch events, actors. But it contains zero real names, zero emails, zero phone numbers. Just callsigns. If someone got access to this vault, they'd learn about vehicles, not about people.

Reporter phone safety

PIN lock keeps the app locked behind a code. If someone picks up the phone, they can't get in.

Panic button triggers an emergency action you configure. Accessible from any screen.

Auto-wipe clears all local data if the reporter doesn't open the app for 72 hours. Nothing to forget, nothing to remember.

Offline mode stores reports on the phone when there's no signal, then sends them automatically once connectivity returns.

Remote device control

From the Security page, operators can remotely suspend (pause), kill (permanently disable), or nuke (wipe everything) a reporter's device. Useful if a phone is lost, stolen, or a reporter leaves the chapter.

Things you'll configure first

Vehicle types are categories like Sedan, SUV, Pickup, Van. You assign types to vehicles so you can filter and sort them.

Concern levels are your escalation ladder. Name them whatever makes sense for your chapter. Set colors for each level.

Dispatch types define what kinds of alerts you send: suspicious vehicle, welfare check, follow-up. Each type gets its own icon and color on the map.

Managing people

Reporters are added by generating one-time invite codes. Give the code in person or over an encrypted channel like Signal.

Operators log in with a callsign and access code. You can create multiple operator accounts and change access codes anytime.

Optional integrations

CarAPI looks up license plates automatically during sightings, filling in make, model, year, and color. Costs about 30 cents per lookup.

Spokeo identifies phone numbers reported through the harassment system. Returns the caller's name, carrier, and whether it's a landline or cell.

Bug reports go straight to the development team. Click "Report Bug" in the sidebar, describe the problem, and it creates an issue automatically. Your data is scrubbed for privacy before it's sent.
